# Being a women in tech
# Problem: there is small number of women in technology
## Rephrase the problem: Technology appeals to a small subset of people, there are more people needed in technology and the situation appears to be getting worse.
## Positives of being a women in tech
  • Being different makes you stand out.
  • Diverse teams means diverse perspectives.
  • Increase in the number of senior women.
  • Lots of opportunities, number of jobs and diversity of jobs is increasing
  • Flexibility and meritocracy.
## Negatives of being a women in tech
  • Campaigns that try to make technology more feminine aren't effective.
  • Career paths unclear.
  • Lack of mentors or role models.
  • Booth babes.
  • Brogrammers stereotype.
  • Imposter complex.

# DO invest in building a network. men and women alike
# DONT forget how much you like programming. you're here for the same reason as everyone else
# DO embrace opportunities to teach or present. TEALs, skillshare, meetups, General Assembly
# DO hold on to your confidence. leave that imposter complex at the door
# DONT forget those classic power poses. think about body language, speak up, lean in
# DO look up, around, and elsewhere for inspiration. you can learn from peers, executives, managers
# DONT shy away from going to events that interest you. meetups, conferences, hackathons, mixers
# DONT drown in stereotypes. brogrammer, booth babe, hollywood images aren't accurate
# DO consider a mentoring opportunity. external programs like iMentor and internal mentoring are great
# DONT underestimate your knowledge. you can speak at a conference or teach. yes, you.
# DO have fun outside of work as well. side projects, exploring arduinos and raspberry pi
